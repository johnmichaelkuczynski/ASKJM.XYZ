KEY THEMES EXTRACTED
====================================================================================================

1. CONTENT INTERNALISM DEFENSE
   - Content externalism is false; mental content determined by descriptive knowledge
   - Twin Earth arguments fail to establish environmental determination of content
   - Concepts are knowledge of existence-claims, not object-involving

2. SEMANTIC EXTERNALISM COMPATIBILITY
   - Semantic externalism (about word meaning) is true
   - Must be sharply distinguished from content externalism
   - Reference is world-relative even though mental content is not

3. ANTI-COMPUTATIONALISM (CTM CRITIQUE)
   - Computational Theory of Mind is fundamentally false
   - Syntax is meaning-how, not morphology
   - Thought requires semantic sensitivity, not mere symbol manipulation
   - Shape neither necessary nor sufficient for meaning assignment

4. NON-SENTENTIAL MENTAL CONTENT
   - Visual perception has analog, non-propositional structure
   - Perceptual content more fine-grained than linguistic content
   - Aesthetic/emotional reactions lack sentential structure yet represent reality
   - Sentential content is derivative, not fundamental

5. SELF-KNOWLEDGE PRESERVED
   - We have Cartesian certainty about our thought-contents
   - Content externalism incompatible with this datum
   - Burge's slow-switching argument begs the question

6. MODIFIED DESCRIPTIVISM
   - Three ways to acquire name reference (ostensive, descriptive, testimonial)
   - Reference mediated by knowledge of uniquely individuating descriptions
   - Kripke cases actually support rather than refute descriptivism
   - Metalinguistic knowledge can simulate understanding

7. ANTI-GRICEAN SEMANTICS
   - Grice's theory of meaning fails
   - Conventional meaning prior to speaker meaning
   - Compositionality refutes Grice
   - Sentence meaning involves success-conditions, not speaker intentions

8. DEFENSE OF ANALYTICITY
   - Analytic truth exists and is not reducible to synonymy
   - Quine's arguments against analyticity fail
   - Two concepts: formal truth (semantic rules) and conceptual truth
   - Non-trivial analyticities don't reduce to synonymy

This work represents a systematic defense of:
